Bhindia

Description

"Bhindi" or "bhindia" is an Urdu and Hindi word for okra. Bhindia is a staple Pakistani household dish made with okra. This semi dry curry is delicious as a side with rice or roti. It is mildly spicy, nutritious, and full of flavours.

History

Bhindia (known as okra) originated from present-day Ethiopia, the mountainous or plateau portion of Eritrea, and the eastern, higher part of the Anglo-Egyptian Sudan. It is said to have travelled with the Bantu tribe who migrated from Egypt around 2000 BC. Soon it was growing along the great river valleys of India and China.

Ingredients

  • Bhindia (okra)
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ⅓ cup oil
  • ½ tablespoon chopped garlic
  • ½ teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • ½ tablespoon ginger paste
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 medium onions
  • 3 medium tomatoes
  • 3-4 medium green chilli

Directions

  1. Wash and dry okra on a towel. Chop off the end and cut the okra in small pieces.
  2. Dry roast okra in a pan on medium heat with constant stirring so the sticky threads burns for 5-7 minutes. We needs the okra to be slightly soft and half cooked. Set aside.
  3. Simultaneously, heat oil in a seperate pan and fry garlic, fenugreek and cumin until lightly golden. Add the remaining ingredients in spices list. Give it all a stir and fry for few second.
  4. Now, add chooped onion and fry until onion are translucent and soft.
  5. Then add the tomatoes, tamarind pulp and sugar. Cover and cook until gravy is thick and oil separates on the sides of the pan.
  6. Next add the roasted okra in the onion tomato gravy and mix well. And cook for 5-7 minutes without covering the lid. Add the slitted green chillies.
